Bob Ross

the Joy of Painting with Bob Ross

Bob Ross was an American painter, art instructor, and television host. He was the creator and host of The Joy of Painting, an instructional television program that aired from 1983 to 1994 on PBS in the United States, CBC in Canada, and similar channels in Latin America, Europe and elsewhere. Ross would subsequently become widely known through his posthumous internet presence.

The Bob Ross Story

Early On

Robert Norman Ross was born in Daytona, Florida, on October 29, 1942 to Jack and Ollie Ross. From his mother Bob learned a love and respect of wildlife. Bob's father was a carpenter and for a short time Bob worked with him but after an unfortunate accident where Bob cut off one of his fingers he decided that carpentry wasn't for him and in 1960 he joined the Air Force.

Learning to Paint

Bob Ross had his first stationed in Florida before being transferred to an airbase in Alaska. It was during his time there that he decided to take his first painting lesson at a U.S.O. Club in Anchorage. To supplement his income in the Air Force, Bob took on a job as a bartender and sold his landscape paintings on gold prospecting pans to tourists.

After leaving the Air Force and returning to Florida, Bob pursued his passion for art by attending various art schools and painting classes. It was during this time that he discovered the "wet-on-wet" painting technique being taught by William Alexander. Bob dedicated himself to experimenting and refining this technique, eventually creating his own unique version. He also developed custom-made paints, brushes, and knives specifically designed for his wet-on-wet approach.

The Bob Ross wet-on-wet technique, also known as "alla prima" or "direct painting," allowed artists to complete their work in a single session. This technique has a rich history in art, dating back to the 16th century. Teaching Others to Paint

Bob's passion for painting and his desire to share it with others led him and his partners to launch the Joy of Painting on public television in 1982. With the television show as a platform, Bob embarked on a journey across America to teach painting to interested individuals. Initially, the classes held in shopping malls and art stores attracted only small groups of students.

However, as the popularity of the TV show grew, so did the live audiences for Bob's public lessons. From 1982 to 1994, he recorded over 400 episodes of The Joy of Painting, solidifying its status as one of the most beloved and well-known shows on public television. Even today, the show continues to captivate audiences.

Bob's dedication to teaching and inspiring others continues to thrive through the countless instructors who carry on his method worldwide. His legacy lives on as they pass on his techniques and share his love for painting with aspiring artists around the globe.

A Media Darling

Although he might seem shy and retiring Bob was a savvy media personality. He often reached out to his viewers for ideas and shared their successes with his audience. He was regularly asked to appear on talk shows where he entranced the live studio audiences. He famously recorded a promo for MTV when the network was still young and growing.

And The Beat Goes On

Bob Ross's influence remains prevalent in today's digital landscape, as evidenced by the multitude of tributes and parodies dedicated to him that can be found across the internet. From Twitter to YouTube, and even within professional sports teams, fan appreciation for Bob knows no boundaries. His universal appeal attracts people from all walks of life.

One remarkable testament to Bob's enduring popularity was the launch of the Bob Ross channel on the interactive gaming platform Twitch. The channel featured a continuous marathon of The Joy of Painting, which garnered an impressive 5.6 million unique views. The overwhelming response included thousands of requests for more episodes even after the series concluded.

Bob's distinctive artistic style has inspired countless imitators, and copycat versions of his artworks are widely available. While Bob generously donated many of his works to public television stations to assist with fundraising, you can still catch a glimpse of an original painting from the show. The Bob Ross Workshop in New Smyrna Beach, Florida, stands as the ultimate destination to appreciate his original pieces. The workshop houses a vast collection of his paintings and offers classes in the Bob Ross method, providing aspiring artists with the opportunity to become certified Bob Ross instructors in landscape, florals, and wildlife painting.
